Who are you REALLY?

Red to Red was a story created for a highschool writing assignment in the mid 2000's. It bears no links to any other Mars missions, Mars exploration companies or anything similar. It's purely mid 2000's fiction. I got overly into the story, as I have habit to do, and wrote what back then was masses of words in a fairly complicated plot about an Australian mission to Mars. Back then I saw this as being a real life thing by about 2010. I actually thought by 2010 we would be heading to Mars. I was wrong. I am slightly disappointed about this, but knowing what I know now, I'm also not convinced that Mars is the best place for us to go next.

I've learned a lot more about Mars, space travel and the challenges. As have plenty of other people who've aspired to put their boots down on a dusty Martian surface. The whole landscape around missions to space has changed hugely in that time. To the point where what I saw as purely government kind of project is now privatised and crowdfunded. The young version of me would have been very perplexed about individuals being rich enough to launch their own space exploration businesses. For now Red to Red is a story about being an Australian who is interested in exploration and space. About asking questions, setbacks and the value of being curious. It's a story and a platform in development and questions, comments and concerns are incredibly welcome!

Amelia (The Author)

Amelia is an educator and STEM communicator with a passion for telling stories and tinkering. After years in dormancy she brought Red to Red back during the very inspiring 50yrs anniversary of the Moon landing in 2019. She wants to make science stories fun and educational, and is loving experimenting with different ways to share narratives. For more information check out her business website here or drop her a line through the contact page!

Please note, this is fictional. A Science, Technology, Engineering and Maths (STEM) story for kids of all ages, we don't actually have the plans, funds, knowledge or tech to make this happen, we just really like the idea! But if you do, go for it!
